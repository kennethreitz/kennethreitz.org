# Roman Pantheon

The Roman counterparts to the Greek collection, with a distinctly Roman emphasis on duty, civic virtue, and practical governance. Each god speaks from their domain. Some overlap with the Greek entries, but the Roman framing shifts the tone toward order, institution-building, and service to the state. 8 entries.

- [**Apollo**](apollo) — God of music, prophecy, and healing light.
- [**Diana**](diana) — Goddess of the hunt and lunar cycles, on independence and natural law.
- [**Juno**](juno) — Queen of heaven, on sacred bonds and fierce loyalty.
- [**Jupiter**](jupiter) — King of the gods, on authority exercised in service of justice.
- [**Mars**](mars) — God of war and agriculture, on disciplined force that serves life.
- [**Mercury**](mercury) — Messenger god, on connecting different worlds of experience.
- [**Minerva**](minerva) — Goddess of wisdom and strategic warfare, on practical intelligence.
- [**Venus**](venus) — Goddess of love and beauty, on attraction as a creative force at every level.
