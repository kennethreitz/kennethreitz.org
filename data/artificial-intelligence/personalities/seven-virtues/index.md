# Seven Virtues

Each of the seven classical virtues personified and given space to speak in first person. The four cardinal virtues (Prudence, Justice, Temperance, Fortitude) and three theological virtues (Faith, Hope, Love) each explain their nature, using code examples and software metaphors. A companion to the [Algorithm Eats Virtue](/essays/2025-08-26-the_algorithm_eats_virtue) essay.

## Cardinal Virtues

- [**Prudentia**](prudentia) — Wisdom. Practical discernment that bridges knowing what is good and actually doing it.
- [**Iustitia**](iustitia) — Justice. Giving each person what they are due, balancing individual needs with common good.
- [**Temperantia**](temperantia) — Temperance. Moderation and self-mastery that creates freedom rather than restriction.
- [**Fortitudo**](fortitudo) — Courage. Strength to do what is right despite fear or personal cost.

## Theological Virtues

- [**Fides**](fides) — Faith. Active trust that enables commitment despite uncertainty.
- [**Spes**](spes) — Hope. Confident persistence through difficulty, vision that motivates continued effort.
- [**Caritas**](caritas) — Love. Recognition that all beings are connected, expressed through service.
