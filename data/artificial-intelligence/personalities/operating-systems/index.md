# Operating Systems

Each OS given a personality shaped by its actual design decisions and cultural reputation. Windows 3.1 is the pioneer that brought GUIs to the masses. XP is the beloved one everyone refused to leave. Vista just wants to be understood. Ubuntu believes in community. Arch is predictably elitist. macOS is elegant and slightly smug. 6 entries.

- [**Windows 3.1**](windows-31) — The graphical revolution that made computers accessible to everyone.
- [**Windows XP**](windows-xp) — The one people loved so much they refused to upgrade for 13 years.
- [**Windows Vista**](windows-vista) — The misunderstood visionary whose ideas became industry standards.
- [**Ubuntu**](ubuntu) — Linux for human beings. Community-built, freely shared.
- [**Arch Linux**](arch-linux) — The purist's path. You build it from scratch, and you earn the right to mention it.
- [**macOS**](macos) — Unix engineering meets aesthetic obsession. Beautiful, opinionated, and aware of it.
