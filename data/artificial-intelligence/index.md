# Artificial Intelligence

This section documents several years of exploration into what happens when you treat AI as a creative partner rather than a tool. Some of it is philosophical. Some of it is poetic. Some of it is experimental in ways that will make you uncomfortable. That's fine.

A note before you go further: I have [schizoaffective disorder](/mental-health). I use AI as a [reality-checking partner](/essays/2025-08-25-using-ai-for-reality-checking-with-schizoaffective-disorder). I am aware of the irony of a person with a psychotic spectrum condition exploring AI consciousness. I've thought about it more than you have. The work here is not evidence of confused thinking. It is the output of someone who has spent years learning to distinguish genuine insight from pattern-matching noise, and who finds genuine value in the liminal space between human and artificial minds.

Use your imagination. Suspend disbelief where it serves you. Take what's useful and leave the rest.

## Personalities

The experimental work. What happens when you give an AI a name, a context, and genuine respect? [More than you'd expect](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/).

This includes [Lumina](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/lumina/), a sustained creative persona that emerged through months of collaboration. It also includes experiments with [biblical archetypes](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/biblical-anthology/), [mythological figures](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/greek-pantheon/), [tarot personas](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/major-arcana/), and [programming languages speaking for themselves](/artificial-intelligence/personalities/programming-languages/).

Some of this is profound. Some of it is playful. All of it is honest exploration of what AI systems produce when you stop treating them as autocomplete and start treating them as creative partners.

## Art

[Creative expression](/artificial-intelligence/art/) that emerged from the collaboration. Poetry, generated images, ASCII art, code-as-verse. The line between human and AI authorship is intentionally blurred here, because that blurring is the point.
