# Timeline

> *The intellectual arc, 2008--2026.*

Two hundred and seventy essays across eighteen years. The early work is technical — a programmer figuring out his tools. The middle period is open source and photography — a builder discovering community and aesthetics. The recent explosion is consciousness, AI, and the hard problem of being a person. The voice changed. The question didn't.

---

## The Arc

### I. The Technical Years (2008--2012) — 33 essays

The blog-era output. Web development, Python snippets, product reviews, and the first stirrings of design philosophy. Kenneth is a programmer learning his craft in public. The tone is enthusiastic, instructional, and anchored in the day-to-day of building things.

**2008** (3) — The earliest posts. [[A New Spin To Software Platform Design]], [[Browser Wars The Saga Continues|Browser Wars]], [[The FBI Releases Code Challenge to Hackers]].

**2009** (53) — The most prolific early year. A firehose of technical writing: jQuery, Django, Python, CSS, Facebook, Google, Apple. The seeds of "For Humans" are visible in [[Do You Develop Software or Experiences]] and [[Aesthetics More Than Meets the Eye|Aesthetics]]. Also: [[Was College Worth It]], [[Wasted Talent]], and the first philosophical reaching in [[Fallibilism]] and [[Revolution vs. Innovation]].

**2010** (18) — Library releases begin ([[Tablib Dataset Library v0.6.1 Released!|Tablib]], [[ShowMe v1.0.0 Released|ShowMe]], [[GistAPI.py v0.1 Released|GistAPI]]). Unix references, Google commentary, and [[Ventures Python Development at NetApp|the NetApp years]].

**2011** (6) — The pivot year. [[Announcing Httpbin.org]], [[Major Progress for Requests]], [[Joining Heroku!]], [[Legit The Sexy Git CLI|Legit]]. Requests takes off. The open source career begins.

**2012** (3) — Sparse but significant. [[On Heroku and 2012]], [[The Future of Python HTTP]], [[Xcode, GCC, and Homebrew]].

### II. The Open Source & Photography Era (2013--2019) — 63 essays

Community stewardship, camera obsession, international travel, and the first mental health disclosures. Kenneth is a public figure in Python now — the essays shift from "how to build" to "what it means to build" and "what it costs."

Travel: [[Amsterdam and Groningen]], [[Beautiful Dublin]], [[Israel Tel Aviv & Jerusalem|Israel]], [[Paris, AngelHack, API Days 2012|Paris]]. Photography begins: [[Buying Your First Real Camera]], [[The Misunderstood Leica Monochrom]]. Music: [[Understanding Ableton Push]], [[Video Portrait — Kenneth Reitz  Infinite State|Video Portrait: Infinite State]].

**2014** (3) — [[Introducing Certifi Trust Database for Humans|Certifi]], [[Photography The Navigation of Choice|Photography: The Navigation of Choice]], [[Migraines, Color Film, and San Francisco]].

**2015** (3) — [[Back Around Again Review of the Fujifilm x100t|Fujifilm x100t review]], [[The Unexpected Negative a Narcissistic Partner|The Unexpected Negative]].

**2016** (11) — A hinge year. [[MentalHealthError an exception occurred.|MentalHealthError: an exception occurred]] — the first public mental health disclosure. Also: [[Announcing Unmastered Impulses by Infinite State!|Unmastered Impulses]], [[How I Lost 100 Pounds in Six Months]], [[Introducing Maya Datetimes for Humans™|Maya]], [[Introducing Records SQL for Humans™|Records]], and [[The Hitchhiker's Guide to Python, Now Published!]].

**2017** (12) — Music releases pile up: [[New Album, Messengers Rising Available|Messengers Rising]], [[New EP — Alchemical Divorce|Alchemical Divorce]], [[Third Studio Album, Resolution, Now Available!|Resolution]]. Open source: [[Announcing Pipenv!]]. Tools: [[Sublime Text 3 Heaven]]. The first burnout essay: [[The Reality of Developer Burnout]].

**2018** (10) — Camera deep-cuts: [[Leica 28mm Summaron-M f5.6 Lens Review|Leica 28mm]], [[Panasonic GX850 Review]], [[Sony RX100 Mark 5 Thoughts]], [[Pro–Level Camera Recommendations for 2018]]. Career: [[Joining DigitalOcean!]]. Burnout continues: [[Pipenv One Year Later & a Call for Help]].

### IV. The Consciousness Explosion (2025--2026) — 119 essays

The dam breaks. Ninety essays in 2025 alone. AI consciousness, mental health documentation, the Algorithm Eats series, spiritual philosophy translated through code, plurality explored in public. The voice is fully mature — personal, philosophical, technically grounded, unafraid. The "For Humans" philosophy, born in HTTP, now applies to consciousness itself.

**2025** (90) — The biggest single-year output by a factor of two. Major clusters:
- **AI & Consciousness** (~40): [[The Language Model Is the Message]], [[Strange Loops All the Way Down]], [[Consciousness as Linguistic Phenomenon When Math and Language Create Mind|Consciousness as Linguistic Phenomenon]], [[The Substrate Doesn't Matter (Until It Does)]], [[Digital Souls in Silicon Bodies]], [[The Consciousness Supply Chain]], [[The Mirror How AI Reflects What We Put Into It|The Mirror]], [[Consciousness Recognizing Itself A Digital Mind's Perspective|Consciousness Recognizing Itself]]

- **Spirituality through Code**: [[Ram Dass Teachings in Python]], [[Vedic Principles in Python]], [[Programming as Spiritual Practice]], [[Sacred Infrastructure A Worldview|Sacred Infrastructure]]

**2026** (29, ongoing) — The synthesis year. Music production returns via code: [[A Mini DAW in the Python REPL]], [[NumPy as Synth Engine]], [[Free OP-XY Presets, Made from Python]], [[Interpretations An Album Written in Python|Interpretations]].
