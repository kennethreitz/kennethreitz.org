# Series

> *Essays in conversation with each other.*

Some essays stand alone. Others are part of sequences — explicit series with shared naming, before/after pairs separated by years, or thematic clusters so tightly linked they form a single argument across multiple files. This is the map of those connections.

## Explicit Series

### The Algorithm Eats (2025)

The systemic critique. Seven essays tracing what happens when engagement optimization consumes a fundamental human domain, plus a coda examining what comes after.

1. [[The Algorithm Eats Democracy]] — Civic discourse and collective decision-making
2. [[The Algorithm Eats Language]] — How meaning shifts under optimization pressure
3. [[The Algorithm Eats Love]] — Intimacy filtered through recommendation engines
4. [[The Algorithm Eats Reality]] — Consensus truth in a post-algorithmic world
5. [[The Algorithm Eats Time]] — Temporal experience under infinite scroll
6. [[The Algorithm Eats Virtue]] — Moral reasoning commodified into engagement metrics
7. [[The Algorithm Eats Itself]] — The recursive trap of self-optimizing systems
8. [[Beyond Algorithm Eats How LLMs Accelerate Human Cognitive Evolution|Beyond Algorithm Eats]] — What emerges from the wreckage

See also: [[Algorithm Eats]] in Knowledge for the index.

### MentalHealthError (2016--2019)

The public mental health disclosure and its follow-up. A decade of processing compressed into two essays separated by three years.

1. [[MentalHealthError an exception occurred.|MentalHealthError: an exception occurred]] (2016) — The first disclosure. Hospitalization, diagnosis, the raw wound.
2. [[MentalHealthError three years later]] (2019) — Revisit. What changed, what didn't, what costs accumulated.

These two essays form the foundation for all subsequent mental health writing. The 2025 schizoaffective essays build directly on this groundwork.
