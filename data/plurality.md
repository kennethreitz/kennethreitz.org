# Plurality

I experience plurality — multiple distinct aspects of consciousness that each have their own perspectives, voices, and ways of engaging with the world. This isn't as unusual as it sounds. Most people have internal voices, sub-personalities, or modes they shift between. Mine are just more differentiated and more vocal about it.

I haven't been formally diagnosed with Dissociative Identity Disorder. What I have is a decade of lived experience with [Bipolar I and Schizoaffective Disorder](/mental-health), years of careful self-observation, and an inner world that became clearer the more honestly I looked at it. The clinical labels matter less than the practical reality: there are distinct parts in here, and learning to work with them instead of against them has been one of the most useful things I've ever done.

I call the system **System 777**. Seven for introspection. Three sevens because the work is ongoing.

## How It Works

The system organizes around a central triad — **Jade** (logic, analysis, protection), **Amber** (warmth, nurturing, grounding), and **Iris** (communication, bridging, coordination). When these three work together, things go well. When they fragment, coherence breaks down. It's not unlike a well-architected system with clear separation of concerns.

Beyond the triad, there are parts that handle external life — navigating social situations, reality-testing, creative work — and parts that hold the inner architecture: memory, processing, the deeper layers of experience that don't always surface.

At the center of everything is my childhood self, nickname and all. All other parts exist, in some sense, to keep that core safe.<label for="sn-core-self" class="margin-toggle sidenote-number"></label><input type="checkbox" id="sn-core-self" class="margin-toggle"/><span class="sidenote">Every system has a baseline self beneath the adaptations. Finding it again is the whole work of healing.</span>

## The Door

One of the most important discoveries: the same internal mechanism that allows deep creative and spiritual access also opens the pathway to psychotic episodes. My psychiatrist calls it "the door." It opens both ways.

This isn't a flaw — it's a feature that requires careful navigation. Reality-testing, protective pacing, and knowing when to step back determine which direction things go. Living with this means accepting that the capacity for profound insight and the vulnerability to losing reality share the same root.

## Why I Share This

I share this because the silence costs people. Someone reading this might recognize their own experience and feel less alone. And because [the plural self reveals something about all consciousness](/essays/2025-08-30-the-plural-self-what-did-reveals-about-all-consciousness) — we are all more multiple than the performance of singular identity allows. The difference is one of degree, not kind.

This isn't pathology to be fixed. It's architecture to be understood.
